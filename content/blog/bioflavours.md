# Biotechnology of Food Additives and Bioflavours

Biotechnology in the food sector utilizes microorganisms to produce value-added products like enzymes, flavour compounds, vitamins, and other essential food ingredients. Instead of relying entirely on synthetic chemicals, the industry is shifting toward these bio-based alternatives.

Food additives are substances incorporated into processed foods on an industrial scale to fulfill specific technical purposes.

**Primary Functions of Food Additives:**

1. **Improve Quality & Nutrition:** Enhances the nutritional profile and functional properties.
2. **Extend Shelf-Life:** Keeps food safer for longer periods.
3. **Enhance Sensory Properties:** Modifies the taste, smell, texture, and visual appeal for better consumer acceptance.

## Types of Food Additives

Understanding the different categories of additives is essential. They act as the building blocks for modern processed foods:

* **Anti-caking agents:** Stop dry ingredients from becoming lumpy.
* **Antioxidants:** Prevent foods from oxidizing and going rancid.
* **Artificial Sweeteners:** Provide intense sweetness (e.g., stevia, aspartame) without the high calories of sugar.
* **Emulsifiers & Stabilizers:** Prevent fats from clotting together and help mix ingredients.
* **Gelling Agents & Thickeners:** Used to build texture and consistency.
* **Food Acids:** Maintain the correct acidic levels for safety and taste.
* **Colours:** Add or enhance color to make food more appetizing.



## Bioflavours and Colors in Detail

Food colors and flavors are critical for the overall eating experience. They are used to:

* Make food more attractive and informative.
* Enhance natural colors or add visual appeal to colorless/"fun" foods (like cake decorations).
* Help consumers visually identify specific flavors.

**Color Theory in Food:**
* **Additive Color:** This refers to light wavelengths reaching our eyes; combining different wavelengths creates diverse colors.
* **Subtractive Color Mixing:** Creating new colors by removing specific wavelengths from a broad spectrum of light.
