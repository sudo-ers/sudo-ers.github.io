# Overview

- **Definition:** Essential nutrients required by the body in very small amounts (hence "micro"), primarily consisting of vitamins and minerals.

- **Categories:** Divided into macrominerals, trace minerals, and water- and fat-soluble vitamins.

- **Source:** Humans must obtain them from a balanced, varied diet because the body generally cannot produce them.

- **Importance:** Critical for growth, immune function, and brain development. Deficiencies can cause severe, life-threatening conditions.

# Vitamins vs. Minerals

- **Vitamins:** Organic compounds made by plants and animals. They can be broken down by heat, acid, or air. Essential for energy production, immune function, and blood clotting.

- **Minerals:** Inorganic elements found in soil or water that cannot be broken down. Essential for growth, bone health, and fluid balance.

# Key Minerals & Their Functions

## Calcium

- **Primary Role:** The main mineral in bones (holding >99% of the body's calcium stores).

- **Function:** Strengthens bones. Because the body constantly regenerates bone tissue, an adequate daily intake is crucial. (Often supplemented alongside Vitamin D and magnesium for better absorption).

## Magnesium

- **Status:** The fourth most abundant mineral in the body.

- **Key Functions:**

    - Helps produce proteins and cellular energy (acts as a fuel source).
    
    - Maintains nerve and muscle function, normal heart rhythm, and blood pressure.
    
    - Acts as an enzyme co-factor (breaks down glucose and fat, regulates cholesterol).
    
    - Strengthens bones.

- **Sources:** Natural minerals (dolomite, magnesite), seawater, salt lakes, and underground mineral deposits.

## Iron

- **Primary Role:** An essential component of **hemoglobin** (red blood cell protein) and **myoglobin**, which transport oxygen from the lungs to the body's tissues and muscles.

- **Key Functions:** Oxygenates the blood, converts blood sugar to energy, supports metabolism and growth, boosts the immune system, aids cognitive function, and maintains healthy skin, hair, and nails.

## Zinc

- **Primary Role:** Catalyzes over 100 enzymes, facilitates protein folding, and helps regulate gene expression.

- **Deficiency Risks:** Higher risk in patients with malnutrition, alcoholism, inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), and malabsorption syndromes.

- **Sources:** Oysters (best source), red meat, poultry, seafood, nuts, whole grains, and dairy. Fruit sources include pomegranate, raspberries, and guavas.

## Copper

- **Human Functions:** Helps make red blood cells, maintains nerve/immune health, forms collagen (for bones and connective tissue), acts as an antioxidant, and helps the body absorb iron.

- **Plant Functions:** Essential for chlorophyll and seed production. Deficiency makes plants susceptible to diseases like ergot.

- **Absorption:** The body increases absorption if dietary copper is low, and decreases it if the body has enough.

- **Sources:** Organ meats, shellfish, fish, nuts, seeds, whole grains, and chocolate.

## Selenium

- **Primary Role:** Part of at least 25 selenoproteins, many of which act as oxidant defense enzymes.

- **Key Functions:** Protects against free radicals and infection (alongside copper and zinc), supports reproduction, thyroid gland function, DNA production, and cardiovascular health.

- **Sources:** Seafood, organ meats, and Brazil nuts are highest. Everyday staples like bread, cereals, poultry, red meat, and eggs provide the bulk for most people.
