# Physiological Disturbances in Alcoholism

- **Definition:** Also known as alcohol dependence, it involves a loss of reliable control over alcohol use. Individuals cannot stop drinking once they start, regardless of the amount or type of alcohol.

- **Key Characteristics:**

    - **Tolerance:** The need to consume more alcohol to achieve the same "high."
    
    - **Withdrawal Symptoms:** Occur when drinking stops suddenly. Symptoms include nausea, sweating, restlessness, irritability, tremors, hallucinations, and convulsions.

- **Scope of Impact:** Even mild to moderate alcohol problems cause substantial damage to the individual, their family, and the community.

- **Short-Term Effects:** Memory loss, hangovers, and blackouts.

- **Long-Term Effects:** Stomach ailments, heart problems, cancer, brain damage, serious memory loss, and liver cirrhosis.

- **Associated Risks:** Markedly increased chances of death from automobile accidents, homicide, and suicide.

- **Gender Differences:** Men are more likely to develop alcoholism, but women suffer worse health impacts even at lower levels of consumption.

# Physiological Disturbances in Drug Abuse

The text outlines the following key factors and physiological impacts associated with drug abuse:

- Ethnicity and culture.

- Socioeconomic status and homelessness.

- Developmental issues and aging.

- **Co-Occurring Disorders:** Demonstrates a bidirectional influence (disorders affect each other).

- **Gender Differences:** Variations in metabolism and drug effects.

- **Organ Impact:** Liver and other organ damage.

- **Heart Impact:** Cardiac-related conditions.

- **Reproductive Impact:** Reproductive consequences.

# Physiological Disturbances in Smoking

- **Major Chronic Diseases:** Causes cancer, heart disease, stroke, lung diseases, diabetes, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), which includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis.

- **Immune and Respiratory Risks:** Increases the risk for tuberculosis and immune system problems (like rheumatoid arthritis).

- **Digestive Issues:** Causes irritation and inflammation of the stomach and intestines, and increases the risk of painful ulcers along the digestive tract.

- **Sensory and Oral Health:** Leads to a reduced ability to smell and taste, certain eye diseases, a higher risk of blindness, and gum disease (periodontitis).

- **Physical Appearance:** Causes premature wrinkling of the skin.

- **Psychological Link:** High levels of negative emotions, such as depression, anxiety, and anger, are associated with smoking behavior.
