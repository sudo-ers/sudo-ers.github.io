# Planning a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is fundamentally essential for maintaining good health and optimal nutrition throughout the human life cycle. Its primary purpose is to protect the body against chronic, non-communicable diseases (lifestyle disorders) such as heart disease, diabetes, and various forms of cancer.

Planning a healthy diet involves providing the body with the exact nutrients it needs every day while staying within an individual's daily calorie goal to maintain or achieve a healthy weight.

## Core Components of a Healthy Eating Plan

A scientifically backed healthy eating plan focuses on balancing macronutrients and micronutrients while strictly limiting harmful additives. It should include:

* **Carbohydrates & Fiber:** Basing meals on higher-fiber starchy carbohydrates, whole grains, and a high volume of vegetables and fruits.
* **Proteins:** Including a diverse variety of protein foods such as seafood, lean meats, poultry, eggs, legumes (beans and peas), soy products, nuts, and seeds.
* **Dairy:** Utilizing fat-free or low-fat milk and milk products.
* **Strict Limitations:** Keeping the diet consistently low in added sugars, sodium (salt), saturated fats, trans fats, and cholesterol.
* **Portion Control:** Actively controlling portion sizes to prevent caloric surplus.


## 8 Essential Tips for Healthy Eating

The syllabus specifically outlines 8 actionable guidelines for planning a healthy diet. You should memorize these points exactly as they are highly likely to fetch full marks in a structured question:

1. **Base your meals on higher fiber starchy carbohydrates:** This ensures sustained energy release and good digestive health.


2. **Eat lots of fruit and vegetables:** These provide essential micronutrients (vitamins and minerals) and antioxidants.


3. **Eat more fish:** Specifically, include at least a portion of oily fish (like salmon or sardines) which are high in beneficial polyunsaturated fats.


4. **Cut down on saturated fat and sugar:** These are the primary drivers of obesity, cardiovascular diseases, and diabetes.


5. **Eat less salt:** Adults should consume no more than 6g of salt a day to prevent hypertension (high blood pressure).


6. **Get active and be a healthy weight:** Diet must be paired with physical activity.


7. **Do not get thirsty:** Proper hydration is critical for water balance and electrolyte function.


8. **Do not skip breakfast:** This helps regulate blood sugar levels and prevents overeating later in the day.
