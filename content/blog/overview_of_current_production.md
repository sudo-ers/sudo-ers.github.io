- **Structure & Challenges:** Highly fragmented and relies on small-scale producers in developing countries. Low literacy and small farm sizes make it difficult to adopt modern practices or meet international food safety standards.

- **Risks:** High numbers of middlemen and handlers increase the risk of contamination and unhygienic exposure. Post-harvest losses are worsened by poor infrastructure (lack of safe water, cold storage, electricity, and transport).

- **Solutions:** Organizing farmers into producer groups, creating public-private partnerships, and using contract farming.

	- _Examples:_ Kenya (fruit/vegetables) and India (spices/gherkins) have successfully used these to help farmers gain capital, skills, and certifications.

- **Green Revolution Legacy:** The historical shift toward heavy chemical fertilizer and pesticide use now clashes with modern international standards that demand minimal chemical use.

# Crop Production

- **Negative Climate Impacts:** There is medium confidence that climate trends have negatively affected **wheat and maize** yields globally and regionally.

- **Neutral/Positive Impacts:** **Rice and soybeans** have seen minimal effects. Warming has actually _benefited_ crop production in high-latitude regions (e.g., northeast China, UK) with high confidence.

- **Extreme Events:** Severe weather events cause immediate, sizable crop damage. While hard to model, some of these events are increasingly linked to human activity.

# Fisheries Production

- **Key Statistics (2010):** Total global production was 148.5 million tonnes (86% for direct human consumption), averaging 18.6 kg per person/year.

    - _Capture:_ 77.4M (marine) / 11.2M (inland)        
    
	- _Aquaculture:_ 18.1M (marine) / 41.7M (freshwater)

- **Socioeconomic Importance:** Vital for global food security. Over 90% of sector workers are in small-scale fisheries, primarily in poorer nations.

- **Climate Assessment Challenges:** It is difficult to isolate the exact impact of climate change on fisheries because it is heavily mixed with other severe factors like **overfishing, pollution, and habitat modification**.

# Livestock Production

- **Research Gap:** Considerably less research exists on how climate trends directly affect livestock compared to crops and fisheries.

- **Core Concepts:** Animal husbandry involves selective breeding and raising livestock to optimize productivity (milk, meat, eggs, draught labor, and disease-resistant cross-breeds).

- **Economic Impact (India Focus):** Crucial for rural survival, supporting the livelihood of two-thirds of the rural community. It employs 8.8% of the population and contributes 25.6% to the total Agricultural GDP.

- **Management:** Effective production requires a thorough initial inventory of resources, including land, feed (crop residues, cereals, forages), animals, water, labor, and capital.


# Food Security and Food Prices

- **Food Security:** Climate change clearly affects food production (and thus security), but quantifying the exact impact is difficult due to non-climate variables.

- **Price Volatility:** While food prices generally fell during the 20th century, they have seen rapid recent spikes.

- **Causes of Price Increases:**

    1. **Demand:** Increased demand for crops for biofuel production.
    
    2. **Economic:** Energy policy mandates and oil price fluctuations.
    
    3. **Policy:** Domestic market reactions, such as export bans initiated by several countries since 2007.

- **Climate Cost Impact:** Studies estimate climate trends caused a **19% price increase** due to supply impacts, though this drops to a 6% increase when factoring in the beneficial yield effects of higher CO₂ levels.
