# Overview

- **Definition:** Nutrients the body needs in large amounts to provide energy, maintain body structure/systems, prevent disease, and function correctly.

- **Components:** Carbohydrates, proteins, and fats.

- **Requirements:** The exact amount needed varies based on individual factors like age, activity level, medical conditions, and health goals.

# Carbohydrates

- **Primary Function:** The body’s main fuel source for muscles, the central nervous system, cells, tissues, and organs. They also help break down protein molecules, prevent ketosis, and regulate blood glucose.

- **Daily Target:** 45–65% of daily calories.

- **Mechanism:** Carbs are sugar molecules broken down by the body into **glucose** (blood sugar).

    - _Storage:_ Excess carbs are stored within cells as **glycogen** (rapidly converted to energy) or converted to fat.

- **Classification:** Essential biomolecules classified into four groups based on monomer units (including polysaccharides). They exist in three types: **starches, sugars, and fiber**.

- **Sources:** Found in all plant foods (grains, veggies, fruits, legumes, nuts) and dairy.

    - _Healthy sources:_ High in dietary fiber and whole grains without added sugars.

# Protein

- **Primary Function:** Uses amino acids to build and repair muscles and bones, make hormones and enzymes, and protect against viruses and bacteria. Maintains metabolic, hormonal, and acid-base balance, and provides tissue structure (cell membranes, organs, hair, skin, nails, tendons, blood plasma). Can also serve as energy.

- **Daily Target:** 10–35% of daily calories.

    - _Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA):_ 0.8 grams per kilogram of body weight (e.g., ~54g for a 150-pound person).

- **Composition:** Made up of chemical building blocks called **amino acids**.

- **Sources:**

    - _Animal-based:_ Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy (these are complete proteins).
	
    - _Plant-based:_ Fruits, veggies, grains, nuts, seeds (often lack one or more essential amino acids).

- **Deficiency:** Occurs when not enough protein is consumed to support the body's needs.

# Fat

- **Primary Function:** Acts as an energy reserve, insulates and protects organs, and helps absorb and transport fat-soluble vitamins.

- **Daily Target:** 20–35% of total daily calories, with **less than 10%** from saturated fat.

- **Types & Sources:**

    - **Good (Unsaturated) Fats:** Monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats lower disease risk. Found in avocados, vegetable oils (olive, canola, corn, soy, sunflower), nuts, seeds, and fatty fish.
	
    - **Bad Fats:** Avoid trans-fats and limit saturated fats.
	
    - **Saturated Fat Sources:** Fatty meats, sausages, butter, ghee, lard, hard cheeses (cheddar), cream, ice cream, savory snacks, chocolate, and pastries.
    
    - **No-Carb Oils/Fats:** Butter, olive oil, coconut oil, canola oil, avocado oil, lard, suet, and tallow.

# Lipid

- **Definition:** Organic compounds that are insoluble in water, including fats, waxes, oils, and hormones. Too much of some lipids can be harmful.

- **Primary Functions:**

    - Form protective cell membranes and control what enters/exits cells.
    
    - Store energy in the form of **triacylglycerols** (stored as fat).
    
    - Act as chemical messengers and make hormones.
    
    - Send nerve signals through the body.
       
    - Help absorb vitamins.

- **Role in Food:** Crucial for odor and flavor development (generating odors/flavors, acting as precursors, or modifying other components).

- **Common Structures:** Cholesterol, oleic acid, triglycerides (a glycerol backbone with oleoyl, stearoyl, and palmitoyl chains), and phospholipids (like phosphatidylcholine).
