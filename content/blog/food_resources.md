# Definition

Food resources are the sources from which humans obtain food. They are mainly classified into:

1. Plant sources
2. Animal sources
3. Microbial sources

---

# 1. Food from Plants

- Plants are the primary source of food and nutrients.
- Humans eat different plant parts such as roots, stems, leaves, flowers, fruits, and seeds.
- Plants provide carbohydrates, vitamins, minerals, fats, and proteins.
## Examples

- **Roots:** Carrot, radish, beetroot, turnip
- **Stems:** Potato, ginger
- **Leaves:** Spinach, cabbage, lettuce
- **Flowers:** Cauliflower, broccoli
- **Fruits:** Mango, apple, orange, grapes
- **Cereals:** Rice, wheat, maize, barley
- **Oil plants:** Mustard, sunflower, castor
- **Spices:** Pepper, clove, cinnamon, cardamom, cumin
- **Sugar source:** Sugarcane

## Importance

- Rich source of nutrients
- Provides energy and vitamins
- Forms the base of the food chain

---

# 2. Food from Animals

- Animals provide food directly or indirectly.
- Animal foods are rich in proteins, vitamins, iron, zinc, calcium, and vitamin B12.

## Examples

## Milk

- Obtained from cows, buffaloes, goats, sheep, camels
- Rich in proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals

## Eggs

- Obtained from hens, ducks, geese, quails
- Rich source of protein and vitamins

## Meat

- **Red meat:** Cow, goat, sheep, pig (contains more fat)
- **White meat:** Chicken and fish (less fat, easily digestible)

## Importance

- Helps body growth and repair
- Provides essential nutrients unavailable in plants

---

# 3. Food from Microbes

- Microorganisms such as bacteria, yeast, and molds are used in food production.

## Uses

- Preparation of bread, curd, cheese, yoghurt
- Production of beer, wine, and vinegar
- Fermentation of foods

## Types of Microbes

- Bacteria
- Fungi (yeast, molds)
- Algae
- Protozoa

## Importance

- Helps in fermentation
- Produces vitamin K
- Improves digestion
- Some microbes spoil food and cause diseases
